## Design Context

### Users
Release owners and senior engineers use Diff Lens while preparing or reviewing a deployment. They need to compare Git states, check whether multiple contributors' branches can land together, and produce review-ready evidence without touching remote refs.

### Brand Personality
Operational, careful, and evidence-first. The interface should feel calm under pressure, dense enough for repeated release work, and explicit about what was checked versus inferred.

### Aesthetic Direction
Keep the existing dark, warm technical workspace: amber/cyan/rust accents, compact controls, clear status badges, and scan-friendly panels. Avoid marketing-style hero layouts for workflow screens.

### Design Principles
- Prefer auditable facts before AI interpretation.
- Keep destructive actions out of scope unless explicitly introduced later.
- Make release blockers visible at a glance.
- Preserve dense but readable controls for repeated operational use.
- Distinguish clean dry-run results from deploy-safety claims.
